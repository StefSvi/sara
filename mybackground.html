My Background

Sara Bloom is a mediator who specializes in resolving complex disputes involving family, friends and finances. She works at the Long Island Dispute Resolution Center and maintains a private practice in Manhattan. She has her master's degree in Negotiation and Conflict Resolution from Columbia University, and has trained under leading experts in the fields of adult family mediation, eldercare, and conflict coaching.


Sara spent 20 years working in the entrepreneurial philanthropic space, helping to found organizations such as Hebrew Public, OneTable and the Areivim Philanthropic Group. Through her work in her family's foundation, she learned firsthand about organizational structures, conflicts, and mergers. As board chair of numerous non-profit organizations and schools, she learned to listen, embrace her greatest critics, prioritize organizational goals, and work collaboratively with staff and board members. She learned about the specific joys and challenges of working in a family setting.


Before this, Sara was a weekly columnist for the The New York Sun, writing about raising her six children in New York City.


Sara dedicates herself to her clients and makes difficult conversations productive. Her aim is to help clients navigate conflict but also develop long lasting listening, collaborating,and negotiating skills that result in independence from needing intervention in the future. Her clients describe her as compassionate, efficient, responsive and practical.
